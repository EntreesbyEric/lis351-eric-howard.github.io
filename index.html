Madison, the capital of Wisconsin, was founded in 1836, 12 years before Wisconsin became a state. According to the 2020 census, Madison has a population of 269,840 making it the second largest city in Wisconsin. Named after the late president James Madison, Madison is home to the University of Wisconsin-Madison. The University of Wisconsin-Madison is the largest univeristy in the state of Wisconsin and is often viewed as an "public ivy" for being a top-ranked instuition in public rankings and research.
